Wildlife SOS


Wildlife SOS, established in 1995 by Kartick Satyanarayan and Geeta Seshamani, began with a mission to rescue India’s dancing bears, who were forced into street performances through cruel treatment. Today, Wildlife SOS has become one of India’s leading conservation organizations, focusing on the rescue, rehabilitation, and conservation of various species, including elephants, leopards, and reptiles. Their work includes conducting anti-poaching operations, managing human-wildlife conflicts, and protecting habitats through land and species conservation projects. Wildlife SOS operates several wildlife rescue centers and sanctuaries, where animals receive medical care, shelter, and rehabilitation. Their public education programs also emphasize the importance of preserving India’s rich biodiversity and minimizing human impact on wildlife habitats.
